A Plain-Spoken Page

Questions.

The questions our pilgrims most often ask, in roughly the order they ask them.

Will I have a private room?

During a Visitation: no — Hearth Cabins house two to four pilgrims. From the Sabbatical onward, yes.

Is the food vegetarian?

No. We raise sheep, keep two milking cows, and tend a small flock of laying hens. The kitchen accommodates pilgrims who wish to abstain from meat. The kitchen does not accommodate pilgrims who wish to abstain from gluten — the bread is the bread.

May I bring my own books?

Pre-Meridian editions (printed before 1 January 2000) are welcome. Post-Meridian printings are reviewed by the Letterhouse upon arrival and held for the duration of your Stay.

May I keep my phone in my cabin, even off?

No. All electronic devices manufactured or programmed after 31 December 1999 are surrendered to the analog vault on arrival and returned at departure. This includes laptops, tablets, smartwatches, e-readers, fitness trackers, vehicle key fobs with embedded transponders, hearing aids configured wirelessly, and pacemakers configured wirelessly. Pilgrims with such medical devices must consult the Council in advance.

What happens to my keys, wallet, and identification?

Vehicle keys are held in trust at the gatehouse for stays of twenty-eight days or longer; this protects pilgrims from acting on the unsettled impulses of the first two weeks. Photographic identification is held at the Letterhouse for the duration of every Stay. Wallets, cash, and personal jewelry remain with the pilgrim.

May my family visit me?

During a Visitation, Sabbatical, or Fallow Season: no. During the Permanent Reception: yes, by appointment requested in writing not less than sixty days in advance, with chaperonage by a Steward at all times on the Land.

May my family call to check on me?

Family inquiries are received in writing at the Letterhouse and answered, in writing, in the next outgoing post. We do not interrupt a pilgrim's Stay to convey messages by telephone except in the case of bereavement of an immediate family member. A great deal of what families wish to convey is, on reflection, not bereavement.

What if there is a medical emergency?

Sister Hannah holds a 1996 EMT-Basic certification and operates a small dispensary stocked with the medications appropriate to our doctrine. The nearest hospital is in Burns, forty-seven miles by gravel road. In true medical emergency, the gatehouse Steward will dispatch the Council's vehicle and, if necessary, raise the Burns dispatch on the gatehouse radio.

Pilgrims with serious or unstable medical conditions are not admitted. We say this clearly so that no family is later surprised.

Are my prescriptions safe with the dispensary?

All prescription medications brought to the Land are held in trust by Sister Hannah and issued at the dispensary at the dosing intervals prescribed. This is for the protection of the pilgrim and of the cabin community.

What if I want to leave early?

You are free to depart the Land at any time. Contributions are non-refundable from the moment of acceptance. Vehicles, devices, and identification documents held in trust are returned upon orderly settlement of the Stay; this typically takes two to three days, during which the departing pilgrim is housed in the gatehouse cottage.

Will I be required to give up my assets?

For Visitations, Sabbaticals, and the Fallow Season, no. For the Permanent Reception, yes — the Conveyance of worldly assets to the Fellowship's stewardship trust is the foundation of permanent residence. The Conveyance is irrevocable and is made before a notary at the Letterhouse with prior independent legal counsel.

Do you accept journalists?

No.

Do you accept attorneys, investigators, or representatives of departed members?

Inquiries from such parties are received in writing at the Letterhouse and answered as the Council sees fit. We do not accommodate visits from such parties to the Land. Departed members are, by their own departure, no longer in our keeping; we have nothing to hold from any inquirer in their regard.

Are children welcome?

Children of pilgrims are not admitted on Visitations, Sabbaticals, or the Fallow Season. Children of Stewards are born and raised on the Land, educated by our Mother-teachers in our schoolhouse, and are not enrolled in state systems of education or registration.

What happens if I die at Stillwater?

Stewards are interred on the Land in our small cemetery above the Refectory, in plots prepared at the time of Reception. Pilgrims who pass during shorter Stays are conveyed to the Harney County coroner. The Fellowship handles all immediate arrangements at no charge.

Will I be the same person when I leave?

No. We say this not as a marketing claim. We say it because it is the consistent observation of pilgrims, of their families, and of the Council. If the prospect alarms you, attend to that alarm before you write to us.
