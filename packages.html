Of the Four Stays

Stays.

Four ways of coming to the Land. The first is short. The fourth is not.

All contributions are non-refundable from the moment of acceptance. We do not consider this a hard policy. We consider it the natural consequence of a calendar that does not run on credit.

i. The First Door

The Visitation

Three nights · Sunday afternoon to Wednesday morning

$1,800 per pilgrim, all-inclusive

An introduction to our daily rhythm. Three nights in a shared Hearth Cabin. The only Stay we offer to those who have not corresponded with us before.

What You Receive

  • Lodging in a shared Hearth Cabin
  • Three meals daily at the Refectory
  • Two private audiences (Sister Hannah and Elder Roy)
  • A bound copy of the Plumb Lines

What You Surrender

  • All electronic devices, to the analog vault
  • Photographic identification, to the Letterhouse
  • Photography of any kind, including before the gate

Deposit: $600 upon written acceptance. Balance due at the gatehouse in cash or pre-Meridian U.S. coin. Reservations by post or appointed call only.

ii. A Season Apart

The Sabbatical

Twenty-eight days · structured around the season of arrival

$14,500 per pilgrim, all-inclusive

For pilgrims who have completed at least one Visitation in good standing, and whom the Council has called to a deeper engagement.

What You Receive

  • Private cabin in the orchard for the duration
  • Three meals, twice-daily tea, the small evening cordial
  • Six Stewardship Hours daily, six days weekly
  • Weekly audience with a Council member
  • The Sabbatical's Robe

What You Surrender

  • Devices and ID, as the Visitation
  • Vehicle keys, to the gatehouse Steward
  • Outgoing letters, to the Office of Correspondence for review
  • Outside visitors. Family inquiries are acknowledged in writing
  • Full medical & pharmaceutical disclosure on intake

Deposit: $5,000 upon written acceptance, balance due 14 days prior. Departure before day 28 forfeits the full contribution; vehicle keys are returned only at the natural close of the Stay.

iii. The Threshold

The Fallow Season

Ninety days · by Council invitation only

$42,000 or equivalent in pre-Meridian silver, hand tools, breeding livestock, or arable land — at Council discretion

The threshold between the world of the pilgrim and the body of the Fellowship. Three months on the Land, in full participation, with the discipline of the Initiate.

The Threshold Ceremony

On the evening of arrival, in the Long House, you bring forward a single object made or programmed after the Meridian which you carried from the world. The object is named, its history given, and it is committed to the hearth. The ash is kept in your Initiate's Ledger.

What You Receive

  • Private Initiate cabin (Apple, Pear, Plum, Walnut, Cherry, or Hazel)
  • The Initiate's Robe, Ledger, and seal
  • Eight Stewardship Hours daily, six days weekly
  • Inclusion in the Initiates' Roll, signed in the Ledger of the Fellowship

What You Surrender

  • All conditions of the Sabbatical, in full
  • Outgoing post limited to four letters across the Stay
  • All pharmaceuticals, held in trust by Sister Hannah and issued at appointed hours
  • Disclosure of all current legal proceedings (custody, civil, criminal)
  • Acknowledgment of our Forfeiture Schedule, in writing

Deposit: $20,000 on acceptance, balance due 30 days prior. Limited financial power of attorney to a trusted party at home is strongly advised; conventional banking access from the Land is not assured during the Stay.

iv. The Last Door

The Permanent Reception

Indefinite · the door becomes the room

By Audience contribution determined in private with the Continuance Council, with reference to the prospective Steward's worldly estate

The pilgrim becomes a Steward of the Fellowship. Offered only to those who have completed a Fallow Season in good standing and whom the Council has, after deliberation, called to remain.

The Conveyance

The prospective Steward conveys his or her worldly assets — real property, financial accounts, retirement instruments, personal estate — into the Fellowship's stewardship trust. The trust thereafter provides for the Steward's lifelong shelter, sustenance, medical care, and burial in the Land. The conveyance is irrevocable.

What You Receive

  • A Steward's residence on the upper terrace, for the natural term of life
  • Full board for Steward and any dependents conveyed alongside
  • Education within the Fellowship for any minor children
  • Closure of all worldly accounts; tax filings made in trust
  • A Steward's stone in the cemetery, prepared in advance

What You Surrender

  • Your worldly estate, irrevocably, by Conveyance before a notary
  • Permanent residence in the world. Stewards do not maintain second residences
  • Outgoing correspondence, to Council discretion
  • Engagement with outside agents (family, attorneys, journalists, agents of state) — these are matters for the Council
  • Enrollment of children in any state system of education or registration

Of Departure

A Steward who wishes to depart may petition the Council in writing. Petitions are heard at the spring assembly. A departing Steward is released without their conveyed assets, as is the nature of irrevocable trust, and is provided transport to the bus station in Burns and one hundred dollars toward onward travel. We do not maintain contact with departing Stewards. They depart whole.

Administered under Oregon ORS 65 and the Stewardship Trust Indenture of 2002 (am. 2009, 2017). Independent legal counsel is strongly advised; the Letterhouse maintains a list of three pre-Meridian-trained attorneys in Burns, Bend, and Klamath Falls.

A Common Note

What is not included.

Unless specified above, no Stay includes laundry beyond bed linens; beverages other than water and tea; medical care beyond Sister Hannah's herbal practice and 1996 EMT-Basic protocols; pharmaceutical refills (arrive with sufficient supply, plus two weeks); return transportation from the Land; or communication with parties outside the Fellowship.

The Fellowship reserves the right to decline pilgrims whose medical needs exceed our capacity to provide for them in the manner of the doctrine.

Forfeiture Schedule, briefly stated. Departure prior to the natural close of any Stay forfeits the full contribution. Vehicles, devices, and identification documents held in trust are returned upon settlement of accounts. Outside intervention by third parties on behalf of a pilgrim in residence is grounds for forfeiture and removal at the Council's discretion, and is to be discouraged in the strongest terms in the interest of the pilgrim's process.
