Of Those Who Have Stood the Season

Testimonies.

Letters voluntarily released for publication. Edited only for length.

I came for two weeks. Three months in, I asked for an extension. Marcus laughed and said, ‘You don't ask for time here. Time gives itself.' That was nine years ago. I haven't owned a phone since the morning I drove in. My family writes; the Council answers when it's right. I am exactly where I am supposed to be, and I will be here when I am laid in.

Hannah K., Steward · entered 2017

We sold the dental practice in Bend in the spring of 2019 and conveyed the proceeds the following month. The first winter Miriam wept for the noise; she had not understood, until the silence, how loud our life had been. Our daughter Esther was born here in 2021. She has never seen a screen of any kind. We are not going back. There is nothing to go back to.

David & Miriam P., Stewards · entered 2019

I knew Marcus before. I worked for him in Bellevue from 1994 to 1998. He was not a calm man in those years. The Land made him calm. I have not stepped past the gate since 2009 and I do not intend to.

Roy V., Elder of the Council · entered 2006

I came in February of 2022 carrying a bag of pills and the conviction that I would be dead by autumn. I am writing this in the spring of 2026 from the cabin called Hazel. Sister Hannah took the pills the first day and I have not asked for them back.

A. (name withheld at request), Initiate · entered 2022

My wife Carol entered the Fellowship in the autumn of 2018 and was received as a Steward in the spring of 2020. Her conveyance included our shared retirement and the proceeds of the home we had owned in Eugene since 1991, which Carol had inherited from her late husband and which was titled solely in her name. After some initial difficulty I have come to accept that this was Carol's choice, freely made and freely kept. I write to her at the Letterhouse twice a year. I receive a card at Christmas in what I believe is her hand.

A letter from Mr. R., Eugene · 2025 · published with the Council's permission

We had not heard from our sister Joanne in nearly two years when the Letterhouse wrote in the spring of 2024 to inform us that she had taken Stewardship and conveyed the proceeds of her late husband's estate. We are grateful she has found her peace and we wish her well in the season ahead.

The Caldwell Family, Eugene · 2024

My grandfather Harold V. came to Stillwater in 2003 at the age of sixty-nine. He died there in 2014 and is buried in the cemetery above the Refectory. I visited him three times across those eleven years and I have visited his grave once since. The estate matter took some years to resolve and I would say only that the Council was within its rights and that I bear no grievance.

A letter from J. V., Spokane · 2023
