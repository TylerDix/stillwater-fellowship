A Letter to the Letterhouse

Apply.

Be plainspoken. There is no advantage in being other than honest with us.

The page below mirrors the postal application we send on receipt of your first letter. Pilgrims who prefer to write by hand are warmly encouraged to do so; a handwritten application is, in our experience, the better predictor of a fitting Stay.

Read by Sister Hannah of the Letterhouse and, where the application progresses, by the Council. We do not share the contents of applications outside the Council. We also do not destroy them.

Of yourself.
If retired, please indicate from what.
Of your circumstance.
We reply by post. Mail to commercial mailbox services is not posted.
A landline is preferred. Voicemail messages are not returned.
Of those at home.
For the protection of pilgrims with complicated families.
Of your health and your matters.
An honest answer is better for everyone. The Council declines pilgrims who are not forthright.
Required for the Permanent Reception. Optional for shorter Stays; some Stays may be subsidized for pilgrims of modest means at Council discretion.
Of your intent.

Your inquiry has been received.

The Letterhouse will respond by post within four to six weeks. We do not acknowledge receipt by other means. We thank you for the patience of the season ahead.

After the Letter

What happens next.

One. Your inquiry is read at the Letterhouse and entered in the day's ledger.

Two. Within four to six weeks you receive our reply by post — either a formal application packet or a brief and gentle letter of decline. We do not explain declines.

Three. If advanced, complete the postal packet, return it with the deposit, and await our confirmation. The Council meets at the new moon; some delay is in the nature of the calendar.

Four. Directions to the gatehouse, an arrival window, and the orientation reading are sent following confirmation.

Five. You arrive on the agreed Sunday between two and four. The gate closes behind you. The Stay begins.
